Process Overview

End-to-End, In-House

Every tool — from the photolithography system to the sputterer and etcher — is designed, assembled, and operated in-house to drive down cost and maximize iteration speed.

01
Chip Design

Inverse-designed AlN waveguide geometries and full GDS layout, optimized for target χ⁽²⁾ nonlinear coefficients and mode confinement.

02
Photolithography

Pattern transfer using our custom-built photolithography system — the world's cheapest scalable apparatus, designed from scratch.

Built for $200
03
RF Sputtering

AlN thin-film deposition via our in-house RF sputtering machine, purpose-built for high-quality parametric film growth.

Target: <$2kIn Dev
04
Reactive Ion Etch

Cl₂-based ICP-RIE defines AlN waveguide sidewalls with smooth vertical profiles using the same RF plasma hardware as sputtering.

Target: <$2kIn Dev
05
Micromanipulation

Precision die handling, alignment, and fiber attachment using our custom-built micromanipulator for nanometer-scale positioning.

Built for <$100
06
Test & Package

Optical probe station tests each die for insertion loss and nonlinear response. Passing chips are fiber-array packaged for system integration.

Materials

Aluminum Nitride Platform

AlN delivers strong χ⁽²⁾ nonlinearity, UV-to-mid-IR transparency, negligible absorption, and direct CMOS compatibility — all in one material that works seamlessly with our in-house processes.

Strong χ⁽²⁾

Second-order nonlinearity for SHG, OPA, and parametric amplification — the foundation of all-optical compute.

Wide Transparency

UV to mid-IR window — the widest of any CMOS-compatible photonic platform available today.

Low Loss

Built-in electromechanical tuning with no added layers, minimal absorption, and low propagation loss.

CMOS Compatible

Direct integration with standard foundry tooling and processes — AlN slots into existing fab infrastructure.

Strategy

Why Vertical Integration

Most photonic startups are entirely dependent on external foundries — paying premium shuttle costs, waiting months for tapeouts, and accepting process constraints set by someone else's PDK.

By constructing our own scalable photolithography system ($200), RF sputterer and RIE chamber (<$2k combined), and micromanipulator (<$100) from scratch, we compress the design-fabricate-test loop from months to days.

Every process parameter is ours to tune. Every cost is ours to cut.
