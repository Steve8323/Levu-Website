Research

The Physics Behind
All-Optical Compute

Six interconnected research tracks — from nonlinear optics to nanofabrication — that together define a complete all-optical compute stack. Every track feeds directly into the Levu XPU.

↓ scroll
Tracks 01–03

The Compute Stack

The core physics and algorithms that enable all-optical matrix compute, memory, and neural network training.

All-Parametric
All-Parametric Optical Computing

Exploiting χ⁽²⁾ nonlinearities — OPA, SHG, and four-wave mixing — to route, amplify, and switch optical signals without any electronic intermediary. Dispersion-engineered AlN brings these processes on-chip.

Neural Networks
Optical Neural Networks

Scalable parametric matrix multiplication in the optical domain using AlN. Inference runs at the speed of light, with hardware-aware training and deep topologies only possible via parametric amplification.

Memory
Optical Memory

All-photonic storage integrated directly onto waveguides, eliminating the energy penalty of repeated O/E conversion. Multilevel cell operation enables in-memory computing with weights read in the optical domain.

Tracks 04–06

Hardware & Fabrication

The physical infrastructure that makes on-chip parametric compute achievable — from interconnects to nanoscale patterning.

Interconnects
Electro-Optic Interconnects

High-bandwidth, low-power EO interfaces replacing energy-hungry copper traces. Terahertz multiplexing and on-chip optical clocking eliminate impedance-matching bottlenecks that limit conventional electronics at scale.

Plasmonics
Plasmonics

Confining EM fields to metal–dielectric interfaces enables optical switching in single-nanometer footprints. Hybrid plasmonic-photonic waveguides balance propagation loss against extreme mode confinement for compact parametric nonlinearities.

Lithography
Photonic Lithography

In-house and foundry-compatible processes for sub-micron waveguide patterning in SiN, TFLN, and AlN. DLP-based projection lithography to single-nm plasmonics, with fabrication-aware inverse design closing the sim-to-fab loop.
